NETRA™ N & T — Project Document

Neuro-Leadership Assessment (Neuroplastic & Techno-Sapient) via EEG + PPG · Understanding, Plain Overview & Technical Plan · Confidential

This document combines our understanding of the requirement, a plain-language overview, and the technical build plan. Part 1 is written for any reader; Part 2 adds the technical detail for the build team.

PART 1 — OVERVIEW Plain language — for everyone

1What we're building

A system that reads a leader's brain activity (EEG) and heart rhythm (HRV, via PPG) during a short guided session, and turns it into two easy-to-read leadership scores — with a clear report, practical coaching tips, and progress tracking over time.

The scoring is a presentation / interpretation layer on top of the device's existing signal pipeline. No new EEG algorithm is required — we read the device's outputs and turn them into the two indices, their meaning, and actions.

2The two traits

N — Neuroplastic (Adaptive Index)

How well the mind adapts, stays calm and recovers under pressure.

Mainly from HRV (heart): SDNN, RMSSD, Stress Resistance, Brain Balance. A strong, direct read.

T — Techno-Sapient (Cognitive-Readiness Index)

How well the person sustains focus and mental stamina in complex, technology-mediated work.

Mainly from EEG (brain): Focus, Brain Fatigue, Mental Pressure. A cognitive-readiness read — not a measure of judgement quality.

Each score runs from 0–100 and sits in one of five stages (Constrained → Emerging → Developing → Strong → Exceptional).

3How it works — four simple steps

1
Wear the headband — the person puts on the sensor headband for a short session (a few minutes).
2
The session runs — a calm/recovery scene and a focus/challenge scene, while the device reads brain & heart signals.
3
Our system scores it — the readings become the two scores (N and T), each placed in its stage.
4
A clear report is produced — plain-language meaning, strengths, growth areas, coaching actions, and the trend over time.

4What the report shows

  • The two scores (N and T), each with its stage
  • A plain-language "what this means" for leadership
  • Practical coaching actions per person
  • A simple brain-rhythm chart of the session
  • Progress over time — improving, holding, or slipping

An AI assistant writes the explanation in warm, personalised, easy-to-read language — different for each person, based on their own results.

5How we keep it honest & safe

  • Every result is a supportive indicator for development — never a verdict or a label.
  • We always say a reading "is consistent with", not that the person definitively "is" that way.
  • We lead with the trend over time; a single session is a provisional snapshot.
  • The scores are checked against expert human judgement in a trial before anyone relies on them.
PART 2 — TECHNICAL DETAIL For the build team

6The 11 readings → two indices

The device provides eleven readings, normalised and combined into N and T:

IndexPrimary inputsContext (not weighted)
NSDNN, RMSSD, Stress Resistance, Brain BalanceFatigue, LF/HF · HR
TFocus, Brain Fatigue (inverse), Mental Pressure (inverse)Relaxation

Emotional State is the 11th reading — held (unweighted) until its polarity is confirmed.

Combining formula (from the spec):

N = 0.30·SDNN + 0.25·RMSSD + 0.25·StressResistance + 0.20·BrainBalance
T = 0.40·Focus + 0.35·(100−BrainFatigue) + 0.25·(100−MentalPressure)

Normalisation: each reading → 0–100 (inverse ones flipped as 100 − value). Example: SDNN 46 → (46−30)/60×100 = 26.7.

10Phased delivery plan

Phase 0 — Setup & data access: project scaffold, DB, auth; device SDK + authenticate; register ingestion webhook; confirm data contract.
Phase 1 — Ingestion & readings: Mode A webhook receive; Mode B (raw) band-power + HRV + artifact rejection + derive composites; quality gates.
Phase 2 — Scoring engine: normalisation, N/T formulas, 5-stage banding; weights/bands in a config file (re-fittable).
Phase 3 — Report generator: Executive report (scores, interpretation, actions, chart, trend); two-layer wording; HTML → PDF.
Phase 4 — AI layer: Claude-generated personalised interpretation & coaching; trend detection; optional coach chatbot.
Phase 5 — VR integration: recovery scene → N, cognitive-load scene → T; capture windows synced.
Phase 6 — Multi-session & platform: session history, trajectory as headline; dashboards; hosting & consent.
Phase 7 — Calibration: ingest 20-participant pilot + human-rater scores; re-fit weights/bands (target weighted κ ≥ 0.6); retain raw signals.

11Calibration approach

  • Starting weights/bands from the spec — the build works immediately without the pilot.
  • Run the 20-participant pilot → machine N/T vs trained human-rater ratings.
  • Re-fit weights/bands (scikit-learn) so the machine agrees with raters (weighted κ ≥ 0.6).
  • Re-derive HRV bands from the cohort's own seated-VR baseline (not resting norms).

12Design principles (locked)

  • Config-driven weights/bands — recalibration is a config change, not code.
  • Two-layer output — "is consistent with", never "is".
  • Trajectory is the headline; a single session is provisional (with a confidence band).
  • Retain raw EEG/PPG for recalibration and audit.
  • Scene-aware — T from a cognitive-load scene, N from a recovery scene.
  • Emotional State held until polarity is confirmed.

13Risks & mitigations

RiskMitigation
Gamma band noisy in VR (muscle artifact)Rely on Beta/Alpha/Theta; treat Gamma as directional only

Composites differ if off the original pipelineDocument as our own implementation; calibrate on the pilot
