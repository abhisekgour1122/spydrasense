GLOSSARY

the technical terms used above, in plain language

🎤 Voice (acoustics)

  • F0 — fundamental frequency = the voice's pitch (high/low), in Hz
  • ST — semitone, a unit of pitch (12 ST = one octave)
  • SD — standard deviation = how much it varies (low SD = steady)
  • drift — pitch slowly creeping one way (e.g. rising under stress)
  • HNR — harmonics-to-noise ratio (dB) = how clear vs breathy/noisy the voice is (higher = cleaner)
  • jitter / shimmer — tiny cycle-to-cycle wobble in pitch / loudness (lower = steadier)
  • RMS — loudness measure · FFT — turns sound into its frequency spectrum
  • spectral tilt — balance of low vs high frequencies; more low energy = warm tone
  • uptalk — pitch rising at the end of a statement (sounds unsure / hedging)
  • syll/s — syllables per second = speaking speed

📷 Face & scoring

  • AU — Action Unit = a standardised facial-muscle movement (FACS); e.g. AU4 = frown, AU12 = smile
  • blendshape — MediaPipe's 0–1 value per facial movement → mapped to AUs
  • Duchenne — a genuine smile (mouth + cheeks together), vs a polite smile
  • σ (sigma) / z-score — how many standard deviations a value is from the person's own baseline (fair across faces)
  • yaw / pitch / roll — head rotation: left-right / up-down / tilt
  • Q1·Q2·Q3 — signal quality: face-valid % · lighting · head-pose in range
  • § — a section number in the NETRA spec documents

🛠️ Tools & validation

  • FACS — Facial Action Coding System: the standard catalogue of facial muscle movements (AUs)
  • MediaPipe FaceLandmarker — the face model (blendshapes + pose + landmarks)
  • OpenCV — computer-vision library for reading & processing video frames
  • eGeMAPS / openSMILE / Praat — standard, peer-reviewed voice-analysis toolkits the acoustic measures are based on
  • κ (kappa) — agreement score between the AI and human raters; ≥ 0.6 = the target before any stakes
  • baseline-relative — every score is read as a shift from the person's own resting sample
Parallel track — Calibration & validation: every threshold is tuned against trained human raters (target weighted κ ≥ 0.6) before any stakes attach. · SpydraSense output is a supportive behavioural indicator — not a verdict, diagnosis, or assessment. It reads expressed behaviour as a shift from the person's own baseline, for voluntary development alongside human judgement.
